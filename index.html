About Me

I grew up in India — earning a BCA then MCA with First Class Honours — before landing my first big break at Capgemini Technology Services. For over two years I worked as a Software Analyst and SAP ABAP Developer, writing enterprise programs that ran inside real organisations. I learned what production software actually means: reliability under pressure, zero-downtime expectations, and collaborating across cross-functional teams of consultants, QA engineers, and project managers.

"At Capgemini I didn't just write code — I learned what it means to own a system that people depend on."

In 2026, I made a deliberate bet on myself and moved to Dublin, Ireland to pursue an MSc in Cloud Computing at National College of Ireland. The goal: take my enterprise engineering foundation and layer modern cloud skills on top — AWS, DevOps, distributed systems, and cloud security. I want to build things that scale.

Between degrees and corporate roles, I also ran a small software startup, delivering custom ERP and billing applications to local businesses. That experience — being the developer, project manager, and client relationship all at once — made me a sharper engineer and a better communicator. I captained my college cricket team too, which taught me more about leadership under pressure than any management course could.
